The weekend

Useful Info

Getting to the venue

The Palauet d’Orsà is set in the Collserola, the lovely hills surrounding Barcelona. It is accessible by public transport or taxi.

Public transport
Take line S1 or S2 from Pl. Catalunya or Gràcia station to Peu del Funicular. Make sure you get on the middle of the train as the first 3 sets of doors do not open at the station (the platform is short!). At Peu del Funicular, walk along the platform and turn left at the top of the stairs to access the funicular, a cable car that takes you up into the hills. Get off the funicular at the top, the station is called Vallvidrera Superior. From there, the venue is a 500m walk away, but up a steepish hill. We would recommend this as a fun way to get to the venue, as long as you don’t mind a bit of a climb the other end! The S1/S2 lines and funicular both run until 2am (last connection leaves Vallvidrera Superior at 1:46am). You can buy tickets from the ticket machines in the metro station. Children under 4 do not need a ticket for public transport. If you'll be using public transport during the weekend it will likely be worth buying a "T-familiar" ticket or a Hola barcelona Travel Card.
Taxi
A taxi to the venue will cost around €15-20. They will be easy to hail from the city centre. There are plenty of apps you can use to book/hail taxis, including Freenow and Cabify. Children do not need a child seat for taxis in the city. You can prebook a taxi with child seats 24 hours in advance by calling: RadioTaxi033. Although they should speak English, if you need help booking taxis, please let us know and we can call for you. Taxis accept cards.

Is there a dress code?

No, wear what you will feel comfortable in! For the Friday evening we will probably be fairly smart, but you don't need to be. On the Saturday think garden party—summer dresses, shirts and chinos. Hopefully it will be lovely and sunny, but it's worth bringing an extra layer as it may get cooler in the evening.

Is there a gift list?

No. Please do not bring us a gift, your being here is all we want! If you still really want to give something, please consider donating to the Meadow House Hospice, a charity very close to Caroline’s heart.

Covid restrictions

If you are arriving from overseas you currently need to fill in a passenger locator form. You must wear a mask on public transport, in taxis, and in healthcare settings (hospitals, pharmacies, etc).

Getting to/from the airport

A taxi to the centre from the airport will be around €30. The metro journey into the centre is slow but cheaper. You have to buy a special airport ticket which costs €5.15. The cheapest way of getting into the centre is using the Rodalies train. It only goes from Terminal 2 and there are two trains an hour into the centre.

Our favourites

We are delighted that you are coming to celebrate with us and, for those of you who are travelling to Barcelona, we are so excited to host you in our home city. We're very happy to tailor recommendations for things to do, places to eat, or where to sit to watch the world go by. Note that for most tourist attractions (e.g. Sagrada Familia) you will need to book well in advance. Here are some of our favourite places in Barcelona.

Restaurants

The following are all within a short walk of the town hall/bar on Friday night.

  • Antúnez — A stalwart. Excellent Catalan cuisine and great, friendly service
  • Fonda Pepa — Ambitious and creative Catalan dishes
  • Jabato—Possibly the best bravas in Gràcia, excellent tapas in a smart neighbourhood bar/restaurant
  • La Pubilla — Catalan market cooking also with a great value set lunch
  • Lluritu — Serious seafood without any pretension, also has a sister bar in the same area
  • Colmado — Cute little tapas bar normally with some interesting veggie options
  • Xativa — The best place to go for a traditional arroz dish

Bars

Again most of our favourites are in and around Gràcia, unless noted otherwise. Most of these places will do some food too. If you're a couple you'll probably be able to squeeze into most places, but if you're a bigger group you'll want to arrive fairly early (e.g. before 8) or book (some below will take bookings) at least on weekends.

  • Vermuteria Puigmarti — Lola is a recognised regular here, a great selection of vermouths
  • La Vermu - Another great neighbourhood vermuteria
  • Bar Salvatge — A natural wine bar with local wines by the glass
  • Raspall — Informal bar (read standing room) where you get a tapa with every drink
  • Croq & Roll — Great range of croquette flavours plus craft beers
  • Bar Roure — Traditional and spacious bar with all the classic tapas
  • Gasterea — Traditional pintxo bar, lively and standing room only but excellent pintxos
  • Bar But — Pretty little bar with some tapas
  • Bodega Quimet — Tiny bodega with great traditional tapas
  • 14 de la Rosa — Our favourite spot in Barcelona for a quiet cocktail or aperitivo
  • Soma — We rarely leave Gràcia, but we will for this place, cute bar/restaurant with a short but good menu in Eixample
  • Bar Brutal — Organic wines and small plates in El Born neighbourhood
  • Dr Stravinsky — Creative cocktail bar with an innovative menu, also in El Born

Brunch/Coffee

For the adventurous, La Pubilla and Fonda Pepa from the list above do Esmorzars a la forquilla—hearty traditional catalan breakfasts often with wine.

  • Little Fern — Kiwi-owned and run brunch spot in Poble Nou not far from the beach
  • Hardware Société — A Melbourne import for serious aussie brunch in Eixample
  • Caravelle — Longstanding antipodean brunch spot with a craft brewery
  • Tiramisu — Italian-run cafe in Plaça de la Vila de Gràcia
  • Nomad Coffee — Ranking roastery and coffee shop in Barcelona, has outposts in El Born and El Raval
  • SlowMov — French-run speciality coffee in Gràcia
  • Syra Coffee — Ever-expanding local speciality coffee chain

Misc

For ice cream, Gocce di Latte in El Born is great with the queues to match, but plenty of vegan options. In Gràcia you can choose from Parallelo (serious ice cream and serious flavours), Anita Gelato (for those with a sweet tooth, looks like something from a Wes Anderson film) or Selvática (more weird and wonderful flavours).

For market shopping, La Boqueria is quite OTT and only populated by tourists, the Mercat de Sant Antoni is a spectacular wrought-iron market serving the Sant Antoni neighbourhood. In Gràcia the Mercat de la Llibertat is a good local option. If you want to pick up jamón, chorizo or fuet to take back with you, Xarcuteria L'Aglà again in Plaça de la Vila de Gràcia is a good bet.
